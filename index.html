Help Wild Cats

Many wild cat species are endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and illegal wildlife trade.

Key Facts

  • There are 38 species of wild cats worldwide.
  • Tigers have lost over 95% of their historic range.
  • Snow leopards are known as “ghosts of the mountains.”
  • Leopards can carry prey heavier than themselves up trees.
  • Big cats are apex predators essential for ecosystem balance.

Help Goats

Uncontrolled goat populations can cause environmental challenges affecting vegetation, soil, biodiversity, and water quality.

Key Facts

  • Goats were domesticated over 10,000 years ago.
  • There are 576 recognized goat breeds worldwide.
  • Mountain goats can climb extremely steep cliffs.
  • Some goat breeds are critically endangered.
  • Goats help control vegetation when properly managed.

Help Sharks

Sharks are among the most threatened marine species due to overfishing, habitat loss, and finning.

Key Facts

  • Sharks have existed for over 450 million years.
  • There are more than 500 shark species.
  • Sharks have no bones — only cartilage.
  • Some sharks must swim constantly to breathe.
  • Shark populations have dropped 70% in 50 years.
